Risk & Reward Simulations

Simulate a year of A/B tests. Find the experiment design that maximizes cumulative true lift.

Experiment programs run dozens of tests per year. Some ideas truly help, some hurt, and many do nothing. Your experiment design — the alpha, power, and MDE you choose — determines which true effects you detect, which you miss, and how much real lift you ship.

Define a portfolio of experiment ideas, set your design, then simulate a full year to see the results. Uses a one-sided test: only positive significant results get shipped.

The Portfolio Lesson

Underpowered tests (low power or high MDE) are cheap per test but miss real effects and let false positives through. Overpowered tests are precise but slow — you run fewer per year. The sweet spot maximizes cumulative true lift shipped, not any single test's accuracy.

Try different designs: lower your MDE to catch smaller effects (but run fewer tests), or raise alpha to ship more aggressively (but accept more false positives). The right answer depends on your portfolio's true lift distribution — and now you have the tool to find it.
